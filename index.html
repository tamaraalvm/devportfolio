Experience

Split

Business Intelligence Analyst Intern

During my time at Split I quickly learned how impactful data truly is. As an intern I was learning material processes and skills while executing multiple projects at the same time. I had a basic understanding of data and how to use it before working at Split. When my internship was over I left ten times the analyst I was when I started. I used multiple platforms including DBT, Snowflake, Fivetran, Google Analytics, Looker, Google Tag Manager, and Salesforce to name a few. My manager taught me how to be resourcesful and look for answers outside of the box. I wrote and edited multiple complex queries, creating essential dashboards that were used by department heads and executives. Presentation skills are often overlooked when learning to become an analyst. My manager worked closely with me to make sure all data presentations were accurate, professional, had action items, and the analysis was understandable to everyone on the team.

ChicExecs PR & Marketing Agency

Marketing Coordinator

While working at ChicExecs, I worked with the marketing team making email campaigns and providing hot leads to the sales team. During my time there I spent time doing data entry in Salesforce and eventually started helping with reporting on Excel. I quickly realized I enjoyed working with data and after expressing my interest to my manager she assigned me to work on a Sales Growth Report, where I analyzed 3 years worth of data for the sales team in order to demonstrate their growth. I further spliced that data into different segments in order to get a better idea of where we were growing and why. For example: number of Account Executives, Jr Account Executives, type of packages sold, and growth by Account Executive. After this I pursued a Data Analytics Apprenticeship with COOP.

Techqueria

Event Coordinator

Techqueria is a community of over 18,000 Latinx tech professionals on Slack. Techqueria hosts the Latinx Heritage Summit (LHM), which is an annual tech conference for the Latinx community and I was part of the planning committee. I worked closely with the sponsors and keynote speakers, sending them contracts, working with them to approve their talking points, and update them on all information. During my time in this role I used the platforms Notion, Slack, and Asana daily in order to track all productivity.
